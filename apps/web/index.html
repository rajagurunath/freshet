The gap

AI sessions contain your best engineering decisions. Right now, they vanish.

Every day your team works through hard problems with Claude Code, Codex, and Kilo Code. The reasoning is sharp, specific, and earned. Then the terminal closes.

That knowledge never reaches the next engineer, the next agent, or the next time the same bug shows up. It is scattered across laptops, tools, and chat scrollback — and it is invisible.

Signal 01

Trapped context

The reasoning that solved a gnarly bug lives in one engineer's local transcript. Nobody else can find it or reuse it.

Signal 02

Fragmented tools

Claude Code here, Codex there, Kilo Code on another machine. No shared memory across the tools your team actually uses.

Signal 03

No institutional memory

New hires and new agents start cold. The decisions your team already made get re-litigated from scratch, every time.

Signal 04

Privacy dead-end

The obvious fix — ship every transcript to a vendor — is a non-starter. Your sessions hold secrets, keys, and proprietary code.

How it works

Capture, curate, share — in three steps.

1

Capture automatically

Context Hub records every Claude Code, Codex, and Kilo Code session into a local store on your machine. Nothing leaves without your say-so.

2

Curate and push

Redact secrets, tag the session, and push the ones worth keeping. Thirty seconds of review, permanent institutional value.

3

Your whole team benefits

Pushed sessions feed a RAG agent, a knowledge graph, a skills library, and PR context — answers grounded in how your team actually works.

Under the hood

How Context Hub works under the hood.

Capture is local. The push is a gate you control. Nothing reaches the company hub until a human approves it.

Local store first. Every session lands in ~/.context-hub on your machine. Capture never touches the network.

Push is a gate. Sessions reach the hub only after a human reviews and redacts. No silent sync, ever.

Self-host the hub. Run the company hub in your own cloud or VPC. Your data stays inside your perimeter.

Privacy & local-first

Your code stays on your machine until you say otherwise.

Context Hub captures locally and pushes nothing automatically. Every session passes through a human review-and-redact step before it can leave your laptop — and the hub itself can run inside your own infrastructure.
